# Risk Assessment Methodology

## Purpose

Define a consistent method to identify, score, and prioritize security risks.

## Process

1. Identify assets, threats, vulnerabilities, and existing controls.
2. Estimate likelihood and impact using defined scales.
3. Calculate inherent risk.
4. Evaluate control effectiveness.
5. Determine residual risk.
6. Select treatment option.
7. Assign owner and due date.
8. Reassess after treatment implementation.

## Scoring Model

- Likelihood: 1 (Rare) to 5 (Almost Certain)
- Impact: 1 (Negligible) to 5 (Severe)
- Risk score: `Likelihood x Impact`

## Risk Rating

- 1-5: Low
- 6-10: Moderate
- 11-15: High
- 16-25: Critical

## Treatment Options

- Mitigate
- Transfer
- Avoid
- Accept

## Governance Rules

- Critical risks require executive visibility and immediate treatment planning.
- Accepted High/Critical risks require explicit sign-off and expiry date.
- Risk register review frequency: monthly for active risks, quarterly for full portfolio.
